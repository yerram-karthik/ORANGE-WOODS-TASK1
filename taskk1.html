Description

A robot arm, also known as a robotic arm or manipulator, is a mechanical device designed to mimic the movements and functionalities of a human arm. It is a fundamental component of many robotic systems and finds applications in various industries, including manufacturing, healthcare, and research. A robot arm typically consists of several rigid links or segments connected by joints, which allow the arm to move and position itself in three-dimensional space. The joints can be revolute (rotational) or prismatic (linear) and provide the arm with degrees of freedom for flexibility and maneuverability.
